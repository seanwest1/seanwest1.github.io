A few weeks ago, I had my car serviced at the dealership.
It was projected to only take an hour or two.
I decided I could sit down and use my phone or tablet for a bit.
I had been reading about networking and security.
So I decided I'd better switch my VPN on just because it's good practice on public networks.
I just picked a WiFi network that was open and used it.
It wasn't fast enough to watch YouTube, so I had a look.
The networks name had extra underscores at the end of it.
I looked for another one, there was the official one, that made you give your email address but was much faster
I was probably using a hotspot that someone had set up.
Most internet traffic is encrypted by default, but if someone knows what they are doing they can extract a decent amount of data about what you are doing.

Mobile data is becoming less and less expensive every year but there are still times when WIFI is needed.

Good public WIFI Habits:
- Turn your VPN on before you join any public network it keeps traffic encrypted even if the network is compromised.  
- Avoid captive portals for sensitive tasks. If a site forces a login/captive page, postpone banking or work.  
- Pause and check the SSID. If two networks look nearly identical, don’t join; a small difference often signals a spoofed hotspot.  
- Prefer mobile data for sensitive actions. When in doubt, switch to your phone plan rather than trusting open Wi‑Fi.  
- Keep automatic connections off. Prevent your device from auto-joining previously used networks without you explicitly approving it.
